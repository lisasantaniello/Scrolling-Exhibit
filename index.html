In the digital age, the concept of 'scrolling' has become an integral part of our daily lives, often dictating our attention, absorbing our time, or directing the order of information we receive. It has become synonymous with non-attention and mindlessness in our fast-paced, screen-dominated world. This exhibit explores scrolling outside of the digital realm, highlighting a collection of physical moments, gestures, and actions that embody scrolling in its many forms. By drawing parallels between our physical and digital interactions, this exhibit invites visitors to reflect on how scrolling has shaped our understanding and interaction with both technology and the world around us.
